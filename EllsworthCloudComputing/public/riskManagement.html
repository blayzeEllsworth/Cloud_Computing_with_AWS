Advantages and Disadvantages of Cloud Computing

Advantages Disadvantages
Faster time to market: Quickly spin up or retire instances, accelerating development and innovation. Internet dependency: A faulty connection might a user's access to information or applications.
Scalability and flexibility: Scale resources up or down without significant investments in infrastructure. Lack of flexibility with vendor: After selecting a cloud provider, it may be difficult to switch or integrate with other systems.
Cost savings: Pay only for what you use! Less autonomy: Users have less discretion over the underlying cloud infrastructure.
Easier for team collaboration: Data is far more accessible, anytime and anywhere with an internet connection. Security concerns: Enhanced risk of data privacy issues or possible online threats; the level of risk is contingent on the provider used.
Data loss prevention: Cloud backups prevent data loss; it backs up the data in the event of an emergency or user error. Unexpected downtime: Downtime still occurs occasionally due to natural disasters or technical bugs.
